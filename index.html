About Me

I'm an undergrad student in Urban & Regional Planning department at KUET with a CGPA of 3.97/4.00. I have strong passion for Geographic Information Systems (GIS), Remote Sensing and Sustainable Urban Development. My academic journey has centered on spatial data analysis, land use planning, and environmental research, supported by hands-on experience with tools like ArcGIS, Google Earth Engine, SPSS, R and Python.

As a Research Assistant at the Center for Environmental Research (CER), I’ve contributed to projects focused on climate change, urban resilience and on related fields. I also completed an internship at Tiller, where I worked on Mouza map scaling and land use zoning, also contributed in "Preparation of Risk Sensitive Database for Core Area of Rangpur and Sylhet District Town (SRDP)”.

Beyond academics, I co-founded an online clothing business, reflecting my entrepreneurial mindset. I have served as team leader in multiple lab-based projects, strengthening my skills in leadership, coordination, problem-solving, and communication under pressure. I’m now actively seeking TA/RA opportunities abroad to contribute to impactful research and continue my academic growth.

In my academic and personal projects, I’ve used various technologies like Python, Google Earth Engine, SPSS, R, JavaScript, AutoCAD, and more.

Research

THESIS: Urban Growth Dynamics and Socioeconomic Drivers in Khulna: A Spatiotemporal Perspective

Technologies Used: Google Earth Engine, ArcGIS, JavaScript, Excel, SPSS, Random Forest

This research aims to analyze the spatiotemporal dynamics of urban expansion in Khulna City during 2000 to 2024 using Remote Sensing and Socioeconomic Indicators (2020–2020): integrating binary classification, spatial statistics geographic distribution tools as well as geographic information systems (GIS).

Key Focus: Urban sprawl, Driving mechanism, PCA model, Khulna city area
