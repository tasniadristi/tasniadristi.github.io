About Me

I am an urban and regional planning researcher with academic and research experience in addressing planning problems through geospatial and statistical tools, technologies, and policy-driven approaches.

My research interests focus on GIScience, remote sensing, spatial analysis, machine learning, and data-driven methods to study urban growth, environmental change, mobility, access to services, and spatial inequality.

At the Center for Environmental Research (CER), I contribute to research on environmental change, air pollution, transportation, and public health using GIS, Google Earth Engine, ArcGIS, Python, and statistical analysis. I also completed an internship at Tiller, where I worked on risk-sensitive spatial databases, land-use zoning, and Mouza map scaling.

I am eager to explore new technologies and interdisciplinary methods in GIScience, remote sensing, climate exposure, environmental health, built-environment vulnerability, and spatial inequality.
